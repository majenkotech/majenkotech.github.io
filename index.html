chipKIT

The chipKIT system started as a rival to the Arduino boards. Based around the PIC32, it rapidly became clear that they would never just replace Arduino, since they are so much more powerful than a lowly Arduino. Instead they have now grown to be a much better platform than the Arduino. Sporting a certain amount of backward compatibility with the Arduino platform to ease migration they now boast an ecosystem of their own.

UECIDE

UECIDE started out as a set of minor modifications to MPIDE, the IDE ported from the Arduino IDE for chipKIT boards. Since then it has grown out of all recognition. It now supports a full range of boards from different manufacturers with vastly different chipsets, from Arduino to Arm, Pic32 to Pi.
